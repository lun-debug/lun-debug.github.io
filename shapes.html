1. The Circle

A circle is a perfectly round shape where every point on the boundary is the same distance (the radius) from the center.

How to Calculate Area

To find the area of a circle, you multiply Pi (π) by the Radius squared (r²).

Worked Example:
If a circle has a radius of 7cm:
Area = 3.14 × (7 × 7) = 153.86 cm²

2. The Triangle

A triangle is a three-sided polygon. In geometry, we focus on the relationship between the base and the vertical height.

How to Calculate Area

The area of a triangle is exactly half of the area of a rectangle with the same base and height.

Worked Example:
If a triangle has a base of 12cm and a height of 8cm:
Area = 0.5 × 12 × 8 = 48 cm²

Common Geometry Mistakes

  • Confusing Diameter (all the way across) with Radius (halfway).
  • Forgetting to use Square Units (like cm²) for area results.
  • Using the slanted side of a triangle instead of the vertical height.
